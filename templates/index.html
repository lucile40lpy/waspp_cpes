DISCOVER STUTEAPOT

The StuTeaPot project deals with pedagogy and psychology, following two main axes. Firstly, it oughts to generate student profiles through their responses to a questionnaire and suggest them tips from students with similar profiles. The second axes consists in exploring the link between students' personality traits and their preferences in learning and teaching methods, with a statistical approach.

StuTeaPot stands for STUdying approaches, TEAching receptivity and PersOnality Traits. While students obtain insights about their cognitive and study styles, the data allows to consider those in perspective of their personality and their reaction to different pedagogical styles.

RESEARCH GROUND

Research in educational sciences and in psychology have a wide intersection. One of the manifestation of this relation is through individual assessment of learning types, may it be with learning styles or cognitive styles for instance. One framework and assessment tool used in this study is Vermunt's Inventory of Learning Styles (ILS). The StuTeaPot test also encompasses questions from the Big Five Inventory (BFI).


Here is a small bibliography of papers related to this topic and used to create the StuTeaPot Test :

  • Vermunt, J. D. (1998). The regulation of constructive learning processes. British Journal of Educational Psychology, 68(2), 149-171
  • Busato, V. V., Prins, F. J., Elshout, J. J., & Hamaker, C. (1999). The relation between learning styles, the Big Five personality traits and achievement motivation in higher education. Personality and Individual Differences, 26(1), 129-140.
